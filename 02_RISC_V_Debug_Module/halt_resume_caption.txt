C1 caption:

"haltreq=1 written to dmcontrol causes
halt_resume_ctrl to assert halt signal
to hart_stub. allhalted remains 0 until
hart_stub confirms by asserting halted=1,
demonstrating correct handshake — DM
waits for hart confirmation before
updating dmstatus."

C2 caption:

"resumereq=1 causes halt_resume_ctrl to
pulse resume for exactly one clock cycle.
hart_stub responds by asserting running=1
and dropping halted=0. allrunning becomes
1 only after hart confirms, showing
correct resume handshake."

C3 caption:

"haltreq and resumereq asserted in same
clock cycle at ~140ns. halt signal goes
HIGH while resume stays LOW — halt wins
as required by RISC-V Debug Spec. This
prevents accidental resume during an
ongoing halt sequence."

C4 caption: 
"At ~180ns hart spontaneously drops
halted=0 without any resumereq from
debugger, simulating an NMI event.
DM immediately detects the falling edge
of halted — allhalted updates to 0 and
allrunning updates to 1 automatically,
showing DM correctly monitors hart state
independent of debugger commands."
